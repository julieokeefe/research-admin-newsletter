  • CTSI Grant Writing Workshop for Researchers: Planning to submit a scientific research grant proposal in early 2019? Need useful feedback on your research objectives? Tufts CTSI invites 10-15 motivated researchers with developed biomedical, clinical, or translational research project ideas to sign up for Developing Page One: a Writing Workshop Series for Researchers. Formerly known as Developing Your Specific Aims, this popular four-session grant writing course is designed to help you develop the critically-important Page One of your grant proposal through peer review, grantsmanship training, and expert support. Sessions start on Wednesday, September 26 at 9:30AM. Participants must attend all four sessions and must be able to submit a draft of their Page One by September 10. Priority is given to faculty members at Tufts CTSI partner institutions. Interested? Apply here by September 10.

New Faculty

Please welcome Dr. M. Elizabeth Fini, who recently joined Tufts MC's Department of Ophthalmology. A native of Greater Boston, Dr Fini earned her doctoral degree at Dartmouth College and did postdoctoral training at Dartmouth Medical School and the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. She is best known for her scientific contributions to our understanding of diseases of the eye. In 2002, she shared the Lewis Rudin Glaucoma Prize from the New York Academy of Medicine for discovery of a novel unifying mechanism for glaucoma pathogenesis. She has also made a significant impact as a mentor to young scientists, many of whom now hold scientific leadership positions at institutions across the United States and the world.
